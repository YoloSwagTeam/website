YoloSwag Team

YoloSwagTeam is an experimental collective whose purpose is to share the maintainership of small FOSS projects to avoid the burden of being alone while doing this and having your users frustrated in result because you aren't always available. Also strength comes from the group.

It is directly inspired from jazzband but mostly differs in the kind of projects and the kind of people in it.

Because together we are stronger.

Implications

Being a member of YoloSwagTeam means:

  • being a maintainer of all its projects at the same level than everyone else
  • yes, that means that you'll be legitimate to code, handle tickets, merge pull requests, make new one and everything else related to project maintenance at the same level than everyone else on : all the projects
  • being an owner of the github organisation
  • bring in your own projects (that matches the requirements) inside of it to share them
  • be involved at your own pace, we all have our life and YoloSwagTeam is here to share to maintainership load, not increase it
  • being a decent human being, respectful, non-elitist, ready to forgive mistakes and teach instead of blame, non-discriminant and having fun

How To Join

If you consider joining, if you haven't done so while reading the whole website to make your decision, please read the Code of Conduct before doing so, you'll be following it.

How to join

  • either open a ticket on the github repository of this website or ask on IRC or Matrix
  • maybe tell a bit about your motivations (can simply be "I want to help")
  • once you are accepted you'll become member of the github organisation and be able to contribute
  • then either send a pull request yourself on this website to add yourself to the member list (or directly push the code if you feel comfortable doing this) or ask someone on the social places to do it for you if it's not something you are used to
  • congratulations, you are now a member of YoloSwagTeam :)

How to include your project

  • first, consider if your project is interesting for this organisation: it must be a FOSS project with users and a certain utility for which you wish to share the maintainership
  • if you have a doubt, just ask other members on the social places
  • you might want to add a specific Guidelines.md to your project to overwrite parts of the default Guidelines.md for some specific situations
  • make sure that the project has a good README and that it's easy to contribute to it, you want us to be all able to co-helper ourselves
  • then, just migrate your project to the organisation
  • and add a reference to YoloSwagTeam in the README saying that it's following to rules of this organisation now (including the CoC)
  • and of course you need to be a member of YoloSwagTeam for that :)

Social places

Right now, two social places exist for the community to interact:

  • an IRC channel: irc.freenode.net#yoloswagteam
  • a matrix room: #YoloSwagTeam:matrix.org

Both are connected using Matrix bridge so you can join either of those depending on your preference and you'll be able to reach the same people.

Guidelines

The first rule of the guidelines is: you are legitimate to co-maintain other projects (yes.)

Here are the general rules that - unless stated otherwise in the Rules.md (if it exists) - you can feel confident to follow:

  • be benevolent, we are all doing this on our free time, we have various levels of knowledge, people will make mistakes, you will make mistakes, we all do, the only people who don't are the ones who do nothing, so: never blame people for their mistakes, fix them, teach them and think about how to avoid that in the future (write tests?).

  • for small bug fixes, typo and the likely, the path is free, just do them
  • for the pull requests of the same kind, the same recommendations applies
  • for minor/trivial features (like add a --debug), if other people generally involved are around check with them if it's ok, if you don't have answers after a few days, feel free to merge
  • for significant features/modifications, try to get the approval of people before merging it and try to get to a consensus, if possible, announce your intent to work on it
  • for big features/modifications/refactoring (like major rewriting), try to get the approval of others before going into it too much
  • again, if no one is around or available for a significant period of time: you are the maintainer, act like it
  • and do what you can when you want, we are all volunteers, the idea is to share to weight of maintainership

Code of Conduct

YoloSwagTeam is dedicated to providing a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of gender, gender identity and expression, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, age, race, or religion. We do not tolerate harassment of participants in any form.

This code of conduct applies to all YoloSwagTeam spaces, including the IRC channel and the Matrix room, both online and off. Anyone who violates this code of conduct may be sanctioned or expelled from these spaces at the discretion of the community managers.
